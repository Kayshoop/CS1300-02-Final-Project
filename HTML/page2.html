Geomagnetic storms are disturbances in Earth's magnetic field caused by solar wind or solar flares. These storms occur when charged particles from the Sun, such as electrons and protons, interact with Earth's magnetosphere. The impact can cause the release of energy in the form of auroras, but it can also disrupt satellite operations, GPS systems, and communication networks. The intensity of a geomagnetic storm is often measured by the K-index, which tracks the fluctuations in Earth's magnetic field. Severe storms can even cause power grid failures by inducing electrical currents in long-distance power lines.
